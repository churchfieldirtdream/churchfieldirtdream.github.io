...Lott stared at me with a matching intensity. The names on this document…they cannot be coincidental. Reading about these events feels like recalling a long-forgotten dream.
I averted my gaze onto Dr. Ruwet. She stood above us, arms crossed, the coffee table separating her from the couch upon which we sat. Her face was motionless, as a parent deciding which punishment is appropriate for their misbehaving child. And like a child I felt. Like a child coming face to face with a hard truth, unable to fully comprehend it.

Tens of thousands of thoughts raced in my consciousness, forming a radio static. isthisadreamisshepullingaprankaretheyworkingagainstmewhoisdarsahwhoislottwhoami A single thought fought its way out and rose to the surface:
“I died here.”

My esophagus was shut tight. I couldn’t breathe. I died here. On this very couch. A memory not so vivid, yet so real. Like a hazy dream. A sharp sensation on the back of my skull, a lack of air, a humid smell, a gurgling sound, a metal door slamming shut. Scraping.
Scratching. Scraping. Scratching.
Scraping…s…cratchng…
